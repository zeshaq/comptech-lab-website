## What's running

A multi-component insurance demo, all on the disconnected lab fleet at
`*.insurance-app.comptech-lab.com`:

- **`is.insurance-app.comptech-lab.com`** — WSO2 Identity Server, the demo's identity
  provider. OAuth2 + OIDC flows, MFA-ready, SCIM provisioning from a user-directory source.
- **`search.insurance-app.comptech-lab.com`** — OpenSearch Dashboards for policy and claims
  search.
- **`redis.insurance-app.comptech-lab.com`** — RedisInsight against the demo's Redis instance.
- **`mail.insurance-app.comptech-lab.com`** — a dev SMTP capture so every outbound email is
  inspectable in a browser. Critical for honest demos of password reset, claim acknowledgement,
  agent escalation.
- **`agent.insurance-app.comptech-lab.com`** — the agentic application, behind the same WSO2
  IS session.

All sit behind HAProxy with DNS records on PowerDNS, identity flows go through WSO2 IS, and
secrets are managed via ESO + Vault — the same posture as the rest of the lab fleet.

## What it demonstrates

- **Identity-fronted everything.** No service is reachable without going through WSO2 IS;
  SCIM provisioning means user lifecycle is centrally controlled.
- **Agentic AI inside a regulated boundary.** The agent has identity-bound tool access; it
  can't reach search or Redis without going through the same OIDC flow as a human user.
- **Inspectable side-effects.** Mail capture means every email the app sends is visible to
  reviewers — critical for compliance walkthroughs.
- **Observable storage.** RedisInsight makes the state store inspectable rather than a black
  box.

Insurance engagements ask for the same things every time: identity that auditors will sign

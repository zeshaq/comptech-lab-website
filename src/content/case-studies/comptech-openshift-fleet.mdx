## Why it exists

Most of our customer engagements ship into **regulated, disconnected environments**. To
keep our advice grounded — and to make engagements faster — we operate a continuously running
reference fleet of our own. It's the system we hand customers a *diff against*, not a
slide deck.

## The fleet

Two clusters, both fully disconnected:

- **`hub-dc-v6`** — the management hub. Runs ACM/MCE, hub-level GitOps, Vault auth integration,
  RHACS Central, cert-manager with internal CA, External Secrets for credential bridging,
  LVMS for storage.
- **`spoke-dc-v6`** — a managed cluster placed via ACM. Inherits its operator install,
  network policy, secret material, and runtime security posture from hub-driven GitOps.

Around the clusters sit the **lab infrastructure** — every VM and service that supports the
fleet without running inside it: MinIO (object storage), PowerDNS, HAProxy edge, GitLab
Enterprise, Vault servers (VM-hosted, not in-cluster), NetBox (host inventory), Quay
(image registry), Nexus (artifact repository), and the oc-mirror workflow that ferries
operator catalogs into the disconnected world.

## Operating model

- **GitOps in pull-mode.** Hub Argo CD reconciles spoke state via ACM-managed ApplicationSets;
  spokes pull manifests directly from internal GitLab over internal networks.
- **Vault as the credential root.** Application workloads receive secrets via ESO; admins
  authenticate against Vault using short-lived dynamic credentials.
- **RHACS for runtime security.** Admission policies, baseline detection, image-risk gates,
  network observability. Integrated with our incident-response loop.
- **OADP for backup.** Routine namespace and PVC backups to MinIO; tested restore drills.
- **Federated GitLab + dual-CI.** Application teams ship through their own GitLab group; the
  platform team operates the two CI build paths — one for application code, one for platform
  manifests — so build pipelines never have ambient platform credentials.

## What's documented

- Architecture decisions (ADRs) for every non-obvious choice
- A complete install manual — see [Greenfield OCP Deployment](/case-studies/greenfield-ocp-deployment/)
- Runbooks per failure mode (lost hub etcd, expired internal CA, drift between fleet and Git,
  OADP restore drill, RHACS scanner upgrade)
- A **history-and-replay log** — what was tried, what failed, why we ended up where we are
- A **site-replication plan** so the fleet can be re-stood-up at a new site in days, not months

## Why it matters for our customers

When you engage CompTech Lab, your platform is built **against** this reference. You get
the install path, the runbooks, the ADRs, and the operating model — minus the things that
don't apply to your environment, plus the things that are specific to it.

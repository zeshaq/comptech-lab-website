Security on a regulated platform is two halves of the same engineering practice: **shift-left**
in the pipeline (SAST, SCA, SBOMs, signed images, policy) and **shift-right** at runtime (RHACS,
DAST, identity boundary controls). We do both as platform work, not as audit theatre.

## What we deliver

- **Shift-left.** Pipeline-integrated SAST/SCA, SBOM generation (Syft), image signing (cosign),
  policy gates (OPA/conftest), Trivy in the CI path, signed manifests in GitOps repos.
- **Shift-right.** RHACS deploy and tune — admission policies, network baselines, runtime
  detection, image-risk gates. Integrated with your incident-response workflow.
- **DAST.** Acunetix or Invicti programs against the deployed app — full crawl, authenticated
  scan, integration with the engineering team's bug queue (not a wall-of-PDFs).
- **Identity.** WSO2 Identity Server or Ping Identity — federation, SCIM provisioning, OIDC
  flows, MFA posture, fine-grained authorization with XACML/Rego where it earns its weight.
  Keycloak when self-hosted lightweight is the right answer.
- **Credential custody.** Vault as the root of trust, ESO bridging Kubernetes Secrets,
  short-lived credentials, dynamic database secrets, transit encryption, audit log shipped to
  your SIEM.

## Engagement shape

DevSecOps engagements typically run alongside a platform engagement, but we also do
**focused identity bring-up** (WSO2 IS or Ping) in 4–6 weeks, and **standalone RHACS tune-up**
work for teams already on OpenShift.
